Basic Diet plans

When focusing on diets for muscle gain and weight loss, it’s important to tailor your nutrition plan to your specific goals. Here's a breakdown for each:

Muscle Gain


1. Caloric Surplus: Consume more calories than you burn.
2. Protein Intake: Aim for about 1.6-2.2 grams of protein per kilogram of body weight.
3. Carbohydrates: Include complex carbs for energy (e.g., oats, rice, whole grains).
4. Fats: Healthy fats are essential (e.g., avocados, nuts, olive oil).
5. Meal Frequency: Eat 4-6 meals per day to ensure a steady supply of nutrients.
6. Hydration: Stay well-hydrated.

Weight loss


1. Caloric Deficit: Consume fewer calories than you burn.
2. Protein Intake: Similar to muscle gain, around 1.6-2.2 grams per kilogram of body weight to preserve muscle mass.
3. Carbohydrates: Focus on fiber-rich carbs (e.g., vegetables, fruits, legumes).
4. Fats: Include healthy fats but in moderation.
5. Meal Timing: Consider intermittent fasting or smaller, more frequent meals.
6. Hydration: Drink plenty of water.
